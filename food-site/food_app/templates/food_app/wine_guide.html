Gabriel's Wine Guide 2021

Cabernet Sauvignon

Where it Commonly Grows:

World's most popular wine is a natural cross between Cabernet france and Sauvignon Blanc that originates in Bordeaux. Wines are concentrated and age worthy. The Rich flavor and high tannins content in Cabernet Sauvignon makes it a perfect partner to rich grilled meats, peppery sauces, and dishes of high flavor.

Alcohol

The average bottle of wine contains 12% - 15% enthanol, contributing to ageability, taste and health.

Tannins

Nautrally occuring polyphenols found in the grape's seeds and skins. Tannins help to to stabalize the wine and buffer against oxidation. Tannins release a gritty or drying sensation that make your lips stick your teeth.

Sweetness

How sweet the wine tastes. The residual sugar left over from unfermented grapes after fermentation occurs.

Acidity

Acidity is what gives the wine it's tart and sour taste.

Body

The body refers to how intense the wine is, from light bodied to full bodied, how full the wine taste from contributing characteristics like aroma and tannins.

Zinfindel

Where it Commonly Grows:

A fruit forward yet bold red that's loved for it's jammy fruit and smoky, exotic spice notes. Originally from Croatia, Zinfandel is related to Croatia's #1 red, plavic Mali. Zinfandel is ideal with Turkish, Moroccan and Arabic cuisine, which bring out the grapes cinnamon like spices. Try it with Asian barbecue!

Sangiovese

Where it Commonly Grows:

Sangiovese is Italy's most planted grape and they key variety in Tuscany's renowned Chianti. It's sensative, tasting quite different depending on where it grows. The high acidity allows Sangiovese to match well with all manners of well-spiced foods. It's one of the few wines that will not get lost when paired with tomato sauce.
